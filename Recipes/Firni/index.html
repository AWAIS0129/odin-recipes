Firni

Welcome to the Delicate Delight of Firni — a creamy, aromatic dessert that brings elegance to every occasion. Made with ground rice, milk, and infused with cardamom, Firni is a traditional South Asian sweet served chilled in clay bowls, often topped with nuts and rose petals. Its silky texture and subtle flavors make it a festive favorite, especially during weddings, Eid, and family gatherings. Whether you're a dessert lover or exploring cultural cuisine, Firni offers a timeless taste of comfort and celebration.

Ingredients

  • Full-fat milk
  • Ground rice (or rice flour)
  • Sugar
  • Cardamom powder
  • Rose water or kewra water (optional, for aroma)
  • Chopped pistachios or almonds (for garnish)
  • Saffron strands (optional, for color and richness)

Steps

  1. Soak a small amount of rice (about 2–3 tablespoons) for 30 minutes.
  2. Drain and grind it into a coarse paste using a little water or milk. (Alternatively, use rice flour).
  3. In a heavy-bottomed pot, bring full-fat milk to a gentle boil.
  4. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
  5. Lower the heat and slowly add the ground rice paste, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  6. Cook on low heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens to a custard-like consistency.
  7. Stir in sugar to taste and cardamom powder.
  8. Add a few drops of rose water or kewra water (optional).
  9. Add saffron strands if using.
  10. Pour the firni into small serving bowls (traditionally clay bowls).
  11. Let it cool, then refrigerate for a few hours until set and chilled
  12. Garnish with chopped pistachios or almonds before serving.
  13. Serve chilled and enjoy!
