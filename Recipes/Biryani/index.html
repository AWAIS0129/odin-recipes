Biryani

Welcome to the World of Biryani — a celebration of spices, aroma, and tradition! Whether you're a die-hard foodie or simply curious about this iconic dish, you've come to the right place. Biryani isn’t just food; it’s an experience. From the royal kitchens of the Mughals to the bustling streets of South Asia, biryani has traveled across regions and generations, winning hearts with every fragrant, flavorful bite. Dive in as we explore recipes, regional variations, history, and the passion behind every pot of perfectly layered rice and meat

Ingredients

  • Rice 1kg
  • Chicken 1kg
  • Oil 300ml
  • Yogurt
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger-Garlic paste
  • Grenn Chillies
  • Spices (turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, cumin, etc.)
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom)
  • Fresh herbs (mint and coriander leaves)
  • Salt
  • Saffron or food color

Steps

  1. Rinse basmati rice 2–3 times and soak in water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, mix your meat or vegetables with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, salt, and a little garam masala.
  3. Let the meat marinate for at least 30 minutes (longer is better for meat)
  4. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and fry sliced onions until golden brown. Set aside half for layering later.
  5. In the same pot, add marinated meat/veggies and cook until tender. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Add green chilies and more spices if needed. Add some mint and coriander leaves for flavor.
  6. In a separate pot, boil water with salt and a few whole spices (like bay leaves, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves).
  7. Add soaked rice and cook until 70–80% done. Drain the rice.
  8. In a large pot, layer the cooked meat/vegetables and partially cooked rice alternately.
  9. Top with fried onions, mint/coriander, and saffron milk or food color for aroma and color.
  10. Cover the pot tightly with a lid. Cook on low heat for 15–20 minutes to let the flavors blend (you can place the pot on a tawa/pan to avoid direct heat).
  11. Gently mix the layers before serving. Enjoy your biryani with raita or salad!
